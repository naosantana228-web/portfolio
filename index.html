AI-Assisted Workflow Adoption

A large operations team needed to adopt an AI-assisted workflow quickly without weakening independent judgment or quality safeguards.

Contribution
Learning strategy, e-learning, decision support, scenario practice
Methods
SME validation, adult-learning design, knowledge checks

Selected project outcome: up to 80% improvement in workflow efficiency.

Read case study

Problem

Learners had to understand what changed, when to consult the AI recommendation, and how to keep their own assessment in the lead.

Approach

I scoped the learning to the new behavior, separated “what’s new” from “what stays the same,” translated the workflow into a memorable decision heuristic, and reinforced it through scenario-based practice and job aids.

Design judgment

The learning treated the AI output as decision support rather than an answer key. Knowledge checks and guided practice focused on observable reviewer decisions, not feature recall.

Result

The portfolio records an efficiency improvement of up to 80% for the selected workflow. The result is presented as a project outcome rather than attributed to training alone.

Problem

Teams in multiple regions needed consistent, self-paced learning across a broad set of policy and regulatory topics.

Approach

I used reusable templates and repeatable course structures to support consistency while allowing content to be updated as requirements changed. Multimedia, interaction, and assessments were aligned to the purpose of each topic rather than added as decoration.

Evaluation

The supporting assessment approach connected clear learning objectives with varied methods, feedback mechanisms, progress tracking, and regular review cycles.

Scenario-Based Policy Practice

A practice experience helped learners move beyond policy recall by identifying violations, making progressively harder decisions, and receiving immediate feedback.

Contribution
Interaction concept, storyboard, scenario sequence
Learning design
Progressive difficulty, feedback, reflection

Design progression: recognition first, then application, feedback, and reflection.

Read case study

Problem

Learners needed practice applying advertising-policy concepts to realistic situations rather than only reading rules or completing a recall quiz.

Approach

I storyboarded an experience that started with simpler identification tasks and progressed to more complex scenarios. Immediate feedback explained the reasoning behind each choice.

Design judgment

The game structure supplied motivation, while the instructional value came from decision practice, increasing complexity, and reflection. The legacy visual treatment is preserved as a historical artifact rather than an accessibility exemplar.

What it demonstrates

Scenario design, interaction planning, visual concept development, and the ability to turn policy knowledge into applied practice.

Process

Structured enough to be rigorous. Flexible enough to move.

I combine the clarity of ADDIE with iterative development practices, keeping stakeholders and learners close to the work from discovery through evaluation.

Analyse

Clarify the performance need, audience, context, constraints, existing data, and the decisions learners must make.

Design

Define objectives, learning flow, practice, assessment, delivery approach, and evidence of success.

Develop

Storyboard, prototype, build, review with SMEs, test with users where possible, and iterate quickly.

Implement

Publish, quality-check, launch, equip facilitators and managers, and support the change around the learning.

Evaluate

Review feedback and performance indicators, identify gaps, refine the solution, and manage its lifecycle.
