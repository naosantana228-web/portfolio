Project framing

Make the mechanics clear, then let the learner practise.

Context

Judgement is difficult to transfer from explanation alone. This concept places learners inside a simulated television experience where they inspect an example, select a label with a custom remote, and learn from the response.

My contribution

I structured the flow from introduction and control onboarding through inspection, decision, feedback, and retry; designed the remote interaction; and used progressive disclosure to keep each step focused.

Key design decisions

  • Introduced one action at a time before presenting the first content example.
  • Used a consistent television and remote metaphor to connect navigation and decision controls.
  • Mapped the three answer labels to distinct coloured buttons for a compact choice interaction.
  • Connected an incorrect choice to explanatory feedback and a retry action rather than a dead end.

Evidence and transparency

The four images are unmodified live captures from the supplied package. The feedback state is verified but not displayed because its rationale contains concrete moderation guidance. Several visual controls expose generic accessibility labels or labels derived from filenames, so no claim of fully accessible implementation is made. No completion, change in behaviour, or business impact result is claimed.

Learning ecosystem

How this solution connects.

The interaction is one part of a performance system. The surrounding analysis, support, and evidence determine whether practice transfers to work.

During

After

Pair practice with accessible guidance, examples, and measurement in the live workflow.
