Additional source assessment. The supplied CepheusSimulation.zip verifies a Storyline workflow simulation comprising four slides, layered states, search and selection practice, text entry, and task navigation. Its screens are not shown because they expose external branding and operational records.

Project framing

Simple, useful, and connected to the work.

Context

Some performance gaps are better solved with support during work than with another course. These resources were designed to make steps, examples, and procedures easy to retrieve and apply.

My contribution

I simplified procedures into clear visual sequences and incorporated realistic cases so employees could connect guidance to the situations they encountered at work.

Key design decisions

  • Kept instructions concise and organised around the user’s task.
  • Used examples to show application rather than repeating abstract rules.
  • Separated support during work from formal training so each asset had a clear purpose.

Evidence and transparency

The public sample demonstrates the performance support format. The supplied Cepheus package provides relevant source file evidence of workflow simulation design, including layered states, controlled input, and guided navigation, but its branded screens and operational records are excluded from publication. No public measure of time saved, error reduction, or adoption is available, so none is claimed.

Learning ecosystem

How this solution connects.

The deliverable is one part of a performance system. The surrounding analysis, support, and evidence make it useful beyond a single learning moment.

Before

Map the workflow

Identify where people hesitate, what information is needed, and where support can appear naturally.

During

Guide the task

Provide short steps, examples, and visual cues without interrupting the work unnecessarily.

After

Learn from use

Collect questions and errors, then refine the resource or add practice where support alone is insufficient.
